Soft Skills

"A boss creates fear, a leader confidence. A boss fixes blame, a leader corrects mistakes. A boss knows all, a leader asks questions. A boss makes work drudgery, a leader makes it interesting." - Russell H. Ewing

Teamwork

I love working on teams. Most people in individual contributor roles that I've met see teams as a negative thing because they can't control their teammates. I couldn't disagree with their analysis more. If all team members are on the same page and work is efficiently distributed, working on a team can lead to huge jumps in productivity. Often in team settings, I take it upon myself to work with members to delegate work in order to maximize the strengths of team members and lead to greater overall output.

Problem Solving

Due to my STEM background, I've been exposed to a crazy amount of problems. Sometimes these problems take a technical form and can involve proving an interesting mathematical result or writing an algorithm for a programming competition. Sometimes though, these problems take less technical approaches. I've solved problems that are part of a case competition as well as problems regarding product development.

Flexibility

As is often the case when developing software, things rarely go according to plan. I've lost count of the number of times that my team has had to completely restart a project due to a framework limitation or that we've had had to change critical features of the project due to changing customer constraints.

Communication

This is one of those skills that I practice multiple times every day. There's a stereotype that software engineers spend all their time hunched over a keyboard coding, but that couldn't be further from the truth. When there are multiple people, sometimes more than a dozen, all working simultaneously on code that interacts at hundreds of points, communication is absolutely critical. This extends outside of immediate teams. Regular communication with clients is also absolutely vital.

Passion

Laziness is not in my vocabulary. For better or for worse, once I decide that I'm doing something, I become extremely passionate about it. When I work on a project, I make it my personal goal to accomplish it more efficiently and effectively than any project I've ever worked on before.
